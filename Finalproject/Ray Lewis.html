Ray Lewis

The Undisputed Defensive Heavyweight Champion

Ray Lewis was a legendary linebacker who played for the Baltimore Ravens from 1996 to 2012. He was one of the greatest defensive players of all time and was known for his intensity, leadership, and work ethic. He was a 13-time Pro Bowler and a 7-time First-Team All-Pro. He won two Defensive Player of the Year awards and was a key player in the Ravens' two Super Bowl victories. Off the field, Lewis was a respected leader and mentor, both in the locker room and in the community. He was known for his charitable work and his commitment to helping others. Ray Lewis has had many notable football achievements throughout his career. He was a 13-time Pro Bowler, a 7-time First-Team All-Pro, and a 2-time Defensive Player of the Year. He was also a 2-time Super Bowl champion and was named the MVP of Super Bowl XXXV. Ray Lewis holds several records in the NFL. He is the Ravens' all-time leader in tackles, sacks, and interceptions. He is also the only player in NFL history with at least 40 sacks and 30 interceptions in his career.

In 2001, Ray Lewis had won his first Defensive Player of the Year award, which is awarded to the best player on the defensive side of the football in the entire league. In 2003, he followed his 2001 year with another DPOY award and also led the league in tackles with 163. Finally, his 2012 year was remakrable since he had been injured mid year, thought to have been done for good, then came back in the playoffs and led the Ravens to their second Super bowl championship.
